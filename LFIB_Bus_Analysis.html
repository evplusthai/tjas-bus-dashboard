LFIB charges 62% more per student yet earns only 6.5% margin — vs TJAS's 68%. The reason: LFIB's COGS/bus is ฿3,668 (vs ฿2,319) and each bus serves only 12.9 riders vs 19.5. Higher price cannot compensate for fewer riders on longer, costlier routes.
Route Distance vs Profitability
Unusually, 120–150km routes are LFIB's most profitable (60% profitable, avg +฿113). Short routes (<90km) are harder — low ridership density fails to offset fixed labor even at shorter distances.

Each active time slot = one trip, utilization = riders ÷ seats for that slot only A bus running 07:40, 14:10, and 16:10 has three separate utilization values: u₀₇₄₀ = riders₀₇₄₀ / seats  |  u₁₄₁₀ = riders₁₄₁₀ / seats  |  u₁₆₁₀ = riders₁₆₁₀ / seats. The average utilization is the mean of active slots only — inactive slots (0 riders) are excluded from the average so they don't artificially drag down the score.

Example — M809 (14 seats, 4 active slots): 07:40→9 riders=64%, 14:10→4=29%, 16:10→4=29%, 18:10→3=21% → avg = 36%. This correctly shows that while AM is strong, the three PM slots are thin.

Key finding: AM averages 58.6% fleet-wide. PM slots decay from 40.5% at 14:10 down to just 13.6% at 18:10. Operating 5 different PM pickup times fragments ridership and progressively empties late buses.
