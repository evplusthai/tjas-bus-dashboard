🔍 Same bus, very different outcomes:
Both schools use 13-seat M-buses. Yet TJAS achieves 70.2% utilization vs LFIB's 38.3%. The gap is structural: LFIB runs 5 PM pickup slots fragmenting ridership, TJAS runs 1–2. LFIB COGS is also 58% higher per bus due to longer routes (90 km vs 69 km).
💡 D-Type Advantage (TJAS only):
TJAS's 39 D-buses (23–30 seats) earn avg +฿2,700/bus at 72.2% utilization with COGS similar to M-buses (฿2,681 vs ฿2,319). LFIB operates zero D-buses — introducing just 4–5 D-buses on high-density LFIB routes could add +฿10,000–฿13,000/term.

✅ TJAS Advantages D-type buses (23–30 seats) are the key differentiator. High capacity means more revenue per route with only slightly higher costs. Single afternoon dismissal time keeps PM utilization healthy. Fleet scale creates route density.
🔴 LFIB Root Problems 5 different PM dismissal times (14:00, 15:00, 16:00, 17:00, 18:00) force the same bus to make multiple low-occupancy PM runs instead of one full run. All buses are M-type (max 14 seats) — no ability to consolidate high-density routes. Average distance 90km vs TJAS 57km inflates fuel cost.
💡 LFIB Priority Actions 1. Consolidate PM dismissal to 2 slots max (e.g., 14:30 and 16:00)
2. Eliminate 7 buses below 8 students — saving est. ฿15K+
3. Consider introducing D-type buses for high-density routes
4. 34 loss-making buses → target 20 through merging
Estimated P&L improvement: ฿25K–35K/term → breakeven or better

COGS per Bus — What Drives the Gap
