Long-Term Fleet & Schedule Strategy
Next semester
🗺️ Reduce PM Slots from 5 to 3
5 pickup times (14:10–18:10) is the core structural problem Each additional PM slot splits ridership thinner. If LFIB can consolidate grade dismissal times to 3 slots (e.g., 14:10, 15:10, 17:10), buses running 3 active slots would see per-slot PM utilization rise from ~25% to ~38%+ — without adding a single new student.
18:10 slot is particularly wasteful: 8 buses for 14 riders = 1.75 riders/bus Negotiating with LFIB to end the 18:10 slot and reassign those 14 students to 17:10 buses (which have spare capacity) would eliminate at least 4–5 buses' worst-performing trips.
🚌 Introduce D-Type Buses
D-buses (23–30 seats) would transform LFIB economics TJAS earns avg +฿2,700 per D-bus per term. If LFIB identifies 3–4 high-density routes (20+ students), replacing 2 M-buses with 1 D-bus per route saves one driver/monitor contract while earning significantly more per route.
Candidate D-bus routes: M168+M169+M177 zone (31 combined students) Three current M-buses in the 80–94km range carry 31 students between them. A single 30-seat D-bus replacing all three would: eliminate 2 drivers, increase revenue capacity per route, and likely generate +฿4,000–฿6,000 profit per term.
📐 Minimum Riders Policy
Set a 13-rider minimum before approving any new route next enrollment No route should launch without confirmed enrollment of at least 13 students. This prevents re-creating PM-only or sub-breakeven buses during the next sign-up season.
GPS audit: 5 routes over 150km need profitability review M173 (167km, +฿1,824) shows long routes can work — but only with 20+ riders. M534 (227km, –฿1,307), M842 (174km, –฿1,230), M952 (159km, –฿271) need minimum 18+ students to break even and should be reviewed each term.
Total savings from all 5 actions: ~+฿31,305 per term Starting P&L –฿23,770 + ฿31,305 recovery = projected +฿7,535 (margin ≈ +3.6%). The fleet can be profitable with disciplined execution of actions 1–3 alone (+฿28,798).
