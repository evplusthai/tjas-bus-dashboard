LFIB Utilization Distribution

Why LFIB Utilization Is Lower The multi-trip structure
⚡ LFIB runs 2–4 trips per bus vs TJAS 1–2 French school has 5 afternoon pickup times: 14:10, 15:10, 16:10, 17:10, 18:10 — reflecting different grade end times. This spreads riders across more runs, dramatically reducing per-trip utilization even when morning trips are reasonably full.
🚨 7 buses run PM-only (0% morning riders) M504, M606, M812, M866, M981, M983, M822 carry NO morning students. These are dedicated PM-only buses — each costing ฿3,100–฿3,500 in fixed COGS regardless of riders.
✅ TJAS fixed this with dedicated 1-trip buses TJAS's 21 one-trip buses run at 65–96% utilization for their single trip, while LFIB PM-only buses average only 14–64% for their single PM run.
🏆 TJAS D-buses are the structural advantage TJAS's 39 D-buses (23–30 seats) generate +฿105K profit on their own. LFIB has zero large buses — all 53 are M-type with 10–14 seats, limiting revenue ceiling per route.

What LFIB Can Learn from TJAS
1. Introduce D-type large buses for high-density routes TJAS earns +฿105K from D-buses alone. If LFIB can identify 3–4 routes where student density justifies a 25–30 seat bus, the economics improve dramatically. A well-loaded D-bus earns 3–5× more than an M-bus.
2. Reduce trips-per-bus from 3–4 to 2 where possible LFIB's multi-trip structure is the primary utilization killer. Combining afternoon pickup times (e.g., 15:10 + 16:10 into one combined pickup) reduces trips from 3 to 2, doubling the effective per-trip utilization from ~40% to ~60%.
3. PM-only buses must be converted or eliminated immediately TJAS also has buses with 0% morning utilization but catches this quickly in performance reviews. LFIB's 7 PM-only buses are running with full COGS (driver, monitor, fuel) for a single PM trip with 2–5 students.
4. Set a firm minimum-riders policy before Term 4 TJAS's breakeven practice (18+ riders for M-type) prevents new loss-making routes from forming. LFIB should implement the same: minimum 13 riders/bus/day before assigning a dedicated vehicle.
