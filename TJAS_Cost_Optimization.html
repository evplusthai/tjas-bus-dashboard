Action 5 — Fleet Right-Sizing & Bus Type Strategy

D-type buses consistently outperform M-type. Optimize fleet composition for Term 4.

Long-Term Strategy
D-Bus vs M-Bus Performance Comparison
Strategic Recommendations
  • 1
    Prioritize D-type buses for high-density areas D-buses (23–30 seats) break even at ~18–20 riders but earn significantly more at 35–50 riders. Where 25+ students cluster in one zone, always use a D-bus over two M-buses.
  • 2
    Reserve M-type for sparse areas only M-buses (10–14 seats) are only justified for truly dispersed areas where student density cannot fill a D-bus. Minimum viable load: 20 students for an M-14.
  • 3
    Replace loss-making M-buses with shared D-bus stops For eliminated M-routes where students live within 1–2km of an active D-bus stop, offer a pickup point adjustment rather than door-to-door service.
  • 4
    Model demand before Term 4 enrollment Use this term's student address data to recluster routes geographically. Use 3km radius clusters — if cluster ≥25 students, assign D-bus; if 15–24, use M-14; below 15, merge into adjacent cluster.
💡 Optimal Target: If all M-buses achieved an average of 22 riders (vs current 19.5), the M-fleet would generate an estimated +฿18,000–20,000 per term instead of –฿14,900 — a swing of +฿33,000.
