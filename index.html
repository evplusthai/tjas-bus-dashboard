📐 Utilization Calculation Method — Per-Trip (Corrected)

Morning Utilization (AM)

AM Util = Riders_07:30 ÷ Seat Count
Every bus has a fixed 07:30 morning run. Riders are counted from the 07:30 column in the Feasibility sheet.
D146 example: 28 ÷ 30 = 93%

Afternoon Utilization (PM)

PM Util = Riders_PM ÷ Seat Count PM slot = 12:15, 14:00, or 15:00 (varies by grade / route)
G-6 and G-9 end earlier (14:00 or 12:15). Most buses use 15:00. Only one PM slot per bus is active.
D146 example: 22 ÷ 30 = 73%

Average Utilization

2-trip bus: Avg = (AM + PM) ÷ 2 1-trip bus: Avg = AM (or PM only) 3-trip bus: Avg = Total ÷ (Seats × 3)
21 buses run 1 trip only. The old method divided by 2 for these, understating their true utilization.
D146 example: (93% + 73%) ÷ 2 = 83%

⚠️ Difference from Previous Calculation

Old formula: Total Riders ÷ (Seats × 2) — always divided by 2, even for 1-trip buses
New formula: Per-trip riders ÷ Seats, averaged across actual trips run
Example — D259 (1-trip AM only, 22 riders, 23 seats): Old = 48% → New = 96%
Example — D251 (1-trip AM only, 15 riders, 23 seats): Old = 33% → New = 65%
